Divvy — Design Concepts

Three distinct directions for the pub-session overhaul. Each shows the Create screen and an Active Tab with 6 people around a redesigned table.

Direction A

Last Call

Warm amber on near-black. Feels like a dimly lit craft pub — intimate, a bit golden. Low-key premium without being stuffy. The system-ui font stays clean; the warmth comes from colour and surface tones.

FeelWarm, intimate. Like the amber glow of a bar at 10pm. Accessible and legible without being clinical.
TablePeople are ~25% larger. Table is ~30% smaller. Amber radial glow gives the scene "lighting." Much less empty.
ChipsFilter chips (Beer / Food / Cocktails / Spirits) sit above inventory pills. Keeps long menus manageable without hiding items.
TypographyDM Sans — friendly, rounded, reads cleanly at small sizes. Keeps the accessible energy of the current font.
Direction B

Neon House

Electric lime on near-black. Pure nightlife energy — bold, a bit cheeky. Space Grotesk gives the whole UI a techy confidence. This direction leans hardest into the "mates out on the lash" brief.

FeelBold, electric, slightly antagonistic in the best way. High contrast. Feels like a nightclub receipt, not a banking app.
TableTable top goes near-black with a lime border — makes it feel like an object in the scene rather than a decorative element. "DIVVY" text branded into it.
You ringDashed lime ring around "you" — very identifiable without needing the word "you" under the name.
TypographySpace Grotesk — techy, confident. Wide and slightly geometric. Makes even numbers feel designed.
Direction C

Smoke & Coral

Deep charcoal with a coral/orange accent. The most "product" feeling of the three — could sit in the App Store alongside the grown-up apps, but still has personality. People are in contained cards instead of floating on whitespace.

FeelPolished, product-grade. Feels like it could be on the App Store next to Splitwise. The coral accent is warm without being soft.
Card seatsEach person lives in a rounded-rect card. Solves the "floating on whitespace" problem by giving everyone a container. Makes the table scene feel denser and more structured.
TableDark surface with subtle grain lines — feels like a real bar table. Coral border at 20% opacity anchors it without being loud.
Trade-offCard seats take more horizontal space — may need SVG to clip to screen edge on very narrow phones. Worth testing on a 375px screen.
